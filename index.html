The Australian Aviation Industry | Effects of COVID-19

a vis by Sachin Fernando

The Big Picture

The domestic airline industry has thrived in Australia due to passengers desire to quickly traverse between business hubs. Australia is home to the 2nd busiest domestic route in the world: Melbourne-Sydney, which sees over 150 daily flights, which tallies up to about 54,000 flights yearly. This section walks through the Pre-COVID landscape.

Australian Airports - Typical Traffic

Effects of COVID-19 on the Industry

COVID-19 has adversely affected many industries in Australia. The continuous lockdown of certain states and cities has had a great impact on Australia’s aviation industry. This following visualisations give the reader an opportunity to compare industry figures, before and after the outbreak began in Australia.

Domestic - Passenger Movements Pre & Post COVID

The following vis shows total domestic passengers passing through an airport. Data from years 2015 to 2019 was averaged to find indicative values for Pre-COVID times. Post-COVID figures (for 2020) are only avaliable up to August, which explains the missing values.

International Passenger Movements Pre & Post COVID

The following vis shows total international passengers passing through an airport. Data from years 2015 to 2019 was averaged to find indicative values for Pre-COVID times. Post-COVID figures (for 2020) are only avaliable up to August, which explains the missing values.

Summary

An interesting thing to note is that during January 2020, Australia saw a 10% increase in International Passengers, and a 5% increase in Domestic Passengers. This means that demand rose when talks of a potential lockdown began. Since then flight activity has dropped to unprecedented lows.
